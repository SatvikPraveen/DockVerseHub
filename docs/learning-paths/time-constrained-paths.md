# Time-Constrained Learning Path: 10 Hours

Perfect for busy professionals who want Docker essentials in a quick sprint.

**Total Time**: ~10 hours  
**Commitment**: 2-3 hours/day for 3-4 days  
**Level**: Beginner to Intermediate  
**Goal**: Understand Docker basics and run applications

---

## Daily Breakdown

### Day 1: Docker Fundamentals (2.5 hours)

**Morning (1.5h)**
1. Read: [Docker Basics](../docker-basics.md) - 20 min
2. Read: [Images vs Containers](../images-vs-containers.md) - 20 min
3. Run: Lab 01 Simple App - 30 min
4. Hands-on: Create your first Dockerfile - 20 min

**Afternoon (1h)**
1. Read: [Docker Cheatsheet](../docker-cheatsheet.md) - 15 min
2. Run: `docker build`, `docker run`, `docker ps` commands - 30 min
3. Review concepts/01_getting_started - 15 min

### Day 2: Multi-Container & Compose (2.5 hours)

**Morning (1.5h)**
1. Read: [Docker Compose Guide](../docker-compose.md) - 30 min
2. Run: Lab 02 Multi-Container Compose - 1 hour

**Afternoon (1h)**
1. Explore: [Volumes & Storage](../volumes-storage.md) - 20 min
2. Hands-on: Create docker-compose.yml from scratch - 40 min

### Day 3: Networking & Basics (2.5 hours)

**Morning (1.5h)**
1. Read: [Networking](../networking.md) - 30 min
2. Run: concepts/04_networking examples - 1 hour

**Afternoon (1h)**
1. Read: [Troubleshooting](../troubleshooting.md) - 20 min
2. Review & practice commands - 40 min

### Day 4: Putting It Together (2.5 hours)

**Full Day**
1. Review all 3 days - 30 min
2. Run Lab 03: Image Optimization - 1.5 hours
3. Build your own multi-container app - 30 min

---

## Key Takeaways

After this 10-hour sprint, you'll know:
- ‚úÖ What Docker is and why it matters
- ‚úÖ How to build and run Docker images
- ‚úÖ How to use Docker Compose for multi-container apps
- ‚úÖ Docker networking basics
- ‚úÖ Debugging Docker applications
- ‚úÖ How to optimize Docker images

---

## Next Steps

After completing this path:
1. Continue with **Beginner Learning Path** for deeper knowledge
2. Explore **Lab 04** (Logging & Monitoring)
3. Move to production with Lab 06
